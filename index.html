Tech Trip

We are hosting a trip of 55 students from Imperial College London, coming from a broad range of departments to participate in a five day-long tour of Israel. Participants will take part in a variety of activities ranging from in-depth high-tech workshops to visits of historical landmarks, not to mention tours of world class campuses.

This unique opportunity will provide the chance to discover the "start-up nation".

Apart from the tremendous educational experience, you will spend five days in one of the world's most historically rich and beautiful countries. Participants will enjoy a myriad of flavors, diverse cultures, vibrant nightlife, and the warm Mediterranean sun that will surely make for an unforgettable trip.

Tech Program

What are the dates?
The program is intended to last 10 weeks, from Sunday 1st of July to Thursday 6th of September. However, we are flexible if those dates do not fit you.
How much does the program cost?
The Israeli society is a non-profit student society, hence the program is free. The only cost you may have is the intern's salary as we believe a student that comes to a foreign country to work for a whole summer should be a minimum paid, however small the amount is. 5600 NIS per month is recommended. As a general rule, the company is free to set the salary depending on the level of experience of the student.
What would the internship require of the companies?
Some mentorship, a chance to work on great projects and an immersion in the israeli work culture is already great.
Are the students undergraduate or graduate? What year?
The program is open to all students in the university, undergraduates to PhD. It is very likely the students joining the program will be on year 2 or year 3 as year 1 lack experience and year 5 are looking for jobs (Imperial College is 3 year for a bachelor + 1 year for a master). It is possible that some PhD students will be interested to come work for the duration of the summer. If you think the positions you offer could fit a relevant PhD student, please let me know so that I can present them as so.
Are you only looking for technology internships?
We focus on this as Imperial College is mainly a technology university, like the Technion in Haifa, and because Israel has a large supply of technology job. However, if you have a marketing or business internship proposition, please let us know. Imperial has recently opened a business school for graduates that is filled with international students from Asia. We can imagine that a business development intern who speaks chinese can be very useful for some israeli companies.
Why are you building a program aimed at non-jewish Imperial College students?
Hence, we built a program tailored to non-jewish people that has awareness of the challenges when discovering the jewish/israeli culture. In addition, going to live in Israel for 10 weeks is very intimidating, so restricting the group to an 'Imperial College' one allows the student to feel they are moving to Israel but within a bubble of their own kind, which makes the experience seem less risky to attempt.
